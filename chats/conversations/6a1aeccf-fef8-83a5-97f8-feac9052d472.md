Uyoung Jeong1
Jonathan Freer2 Seungryul Baek1 Hyung Jin Chang2 Kwang In Kim3
1UNIST 2University of Birmingham 3POSTECH
Abstract
We study multi-dataset training (MDT) for pose estimation,
where skeletal heterogeneity presents a unique challenge
that existing methods have yet to address. In traditional
domains, e.g. regression and classification, MDT typically
relies on dataset merging or multi-head supervision. How￾ever, the diversity of skeleton types and limited cross-dataset
supervision complicate integration in pose estimation. To
address these challenges, we introduce PoseBH, a new MDT
framework that tackles keypoint heterogeneity and limited
supervision through two key techniques. First, we pro￾pose nonparametric keypoint prototypes that learn within
a unified embedding space, enabling seamless integration
across skeleton types. Second, we develop a cross-type
self-supervision mechanism that aligns keypoint predictions
with keypoint embedding prototypes, providing supervision
without relying on teacher-student models or additional
augmentations. PoseBH substantially improves generaliza￾tion across whole-body and animal pose datasets, includ￾ing COCO-WholeBody, AP-10K, and APT-36K, while pre￾serving performance on standard human pose benchmarks
(COCO, MPII, and AIC). Furthermore, our learned key￾point embeddings transfer effectively to hand shape esti￾mation (InterHand2.6M) and human body shape estimation
(3DPW). The code for PoseBH is available at: https:
//github.com/uyoung-jeong/PoseBH.

tracking [13, 45, 49]. These domains pose distinct oper￾ational challenges, often necessitating significant domain
adaptation for robust pose estimation. To address this, we
aim to train a human pose estimator that generalizes effec￾tively across multiple datasets.
However, multi-dataset training (MDT) for pose es￾timation presents two key challenges. First, keypoints

or input augmentation. As a result, we provide effective
supervision for underrepresented and discrepant datasets,

Our main technical contributions are as follows:
• Keypoint prototypes for learning arbitrary keypoints from
multiple datasets ensuring high transferability.
• A cross-type self-supervision mechanism that aligns key￾point regression outputs with keypoint embeddings, en￾riching supervision for unlabeled keypoints.
• Strong generalization across estimation tasks, including
whole-body, animal, and hand pose estimation, as well as
human shape estimation.
2. Related works
Multi-dataset training has been widely explored in vari￾ous computer vision tasks. Relabeling and pseudo-labeling
are among the most straightforward yet resource-intensive
approaches. For example, MSeg [20] employed relabeling
for segmentation, while [68] used offline pseudo-labeling
for object detection. However, both methods face scalability
challenges, as adding a new dataset requires reprocessing
annotations for all previously processed datasets to redefine
the class set, leading to a quadratic computational cost.
Several approaches have been proposed to improve scal￾ability. In object detection, [4, 70] aim to unify object class
labels by merging similar categories. However, they strug￾gle to integrate localization features of the same object
due to the absence of spatial information in class logits.
UniTrack learns a unified identity embedding through self￾supervised learning for multiple object-tracking tasks, in￾cluding human pose tracking [51]. However, it prioritizes
pose propagation over estimation and relies exclusively on
the PoseTrack18 [2] skeleton, without incorporating multi￾ple pose datasets.
Another class of methods [50, 54, 58, 66] em￾ploys network modules, such as graph convolutional net￾works (GCN), for cross-dataset adaptation. However, these
dataset-specific modules limit domain transferability, as
their input and output sizes are dictated by the label struc￾ture, making them incompatible with datasets that have dif￾ferent keypoint formats. In contrast, our approach achieves
flexible transferability without relying on dataset-specific
domain adaptation modules.
Human pose estimation with MDT. In 3D human shape
estimation, several methods have used pseudo-ground truths
(GTs) generated by the SMPL model for MDT [5, 18,
43, 44]. However, these pseudo-GTs are often inaccu￾rate due to depth ambiguity in 2D images and the lim￾ited expressiveness of the parametric 3D model [30, 31].
Similar to [70], Sar´ andi et al.’s affine-combining autoen- ´
coder (ACAE) merges 3D joint coordinates via an autoen￾coder [37]. However, the autoencoder’s learnable parame￾ters are dataset-dependent, limiting its generalizability. Fur￾thermore, ACAE does not preserve pixel-level features,
making it unsuitable when spatial information is crucial.
Recent works on category-agnostic pose estimation inte￾grate various objects, such as chairs and vehicles, by com￾bining multiple datasets [14, 55]. These methods follow a
relabeling approach, creating a superset of keypoint classes
and detecting only the keypoints defined in their relabeled
dataset. As a result, they do not address keypoint hetero￾geneity or label sparsity issues, as training and evalua￾tion occur within a single labeled dataset. In contrast, our
method uses multiple skeletons to improve generalization
across diverse downstream domains.
In 2D human pose estimation, UniHCP performs mul￾tiple human perception tasks using task-specific queries
without incorporating task-specific layers inside the back￾bone [6]. This design limits its ability to generalize to dis￾tant domains such as animals and whole-body pose estima￾tion. ViTPose++ [56] and Sapiens [17] employ multi-head
architectures for MDT but do not address skeleton hetero￾geneity or the resulting label sparsity.

Prototype learning can be interpreted as proxy-based
metric learning [36], where the centers of a fixed set of clus￾ters serve as prototypes. This approach has been studied in
various domains, including semantic segmentation [8, 10,
69], person re-identification [33], meta-learning [25, 40, 61,
65], and visual interpretability [3, 9, 48].
ProtoSeg [69] introduces a nonparametric approach to
semantic segmentation by assigning multiple prototypes per
class. ProMotion [28] employs prototypes to unify scene
depth estimation and optical flow tasks. While our method
draws inspiration from ProtoSeg and aligns with ProMo￾tion in exploiting the benefits of prototypes for MDT, it
differs in three key aspects. First, our approach applies
keypoint-level supervision, which is inherently sparser than
segmentation, depth, or optical flow. Second, we specifi￾cally address skeleton heterogeneity and cross-dataset rela￾tionships, aspects not considered by ProtoSeg and ProMo￾tion. Third, while ProtoSeg and ProMotion apply fully su￾pervised learning, our method tackles the challenge of un￾labeled data, a problem unique to MDT pose estimation.

4. Experiments
4.1. Experimental setup
We follow the experimental setup of ViTPose++ [56], using
six human pose datasets for training. (1) COCO consists of
57,000 training and 5,000 validation images, annotated with
17 keypoints [24]. (2) AIChallenger (AIC) contains 210,000
training and 30,000 validation images, annotated with 14
keypoints [52]. (3) MPII includes approximately 15,000
training, 2,729 validation, and 5,700 test images, with 16
keypoints [1]. (4) AP-10K is an animal pose dataset with
approximately 7,000 training, 1,000 validation, and 2,000
test images [62]. (5) APT-36K, a video-based animal pose
dataset, consists of about 25,000 training, 3,600 validation,
and 7,000 test images [60]. Both AP-10K and APT-36K
share the same skeletal format with 17 keypoints, largely
following COCO. (6) COCO-WholeBody extends COCO
with whole-body annotations covering 133 keypoints [52].
We follow standard evaluation protocols, using the average
precision (AP) metric based on object keypoint similarity
(OKS) for COCO, AIC, COCO-WholeBody, AP-10K, and
APT-36K. For MPII, the percentage of correct keypoints
(PCK) metric is used [1].
For the domain transfer experiments, we use Inter￾Hand2.6M [29] and 3DPW [47]. InterHand2.6M is a two￾hand pose estimation dataset containing approximately
2.6M frames, annotated with 21 keypoints. We adopt the
ViTPose++ configuration for frame sampling and dataset
splits (training, validation, and test). For evaluation, we use
PCK, AUC, and EPE, consistent with ViTPose++. 3DPW
is an in-the-wild 3D human shape estimation dataset with
18,000 training and 26,000 test frames, annotated with 24
keypoints. We use COCO-style AP and AR as evaluation
metrics. In both InterHand2.6M and 3DPW, the projected
2D keypoints are used as ground-truths.
Further details on the experimental setups, the impact of
varying hyperparameters, and failure case analysis can be
found in the supplemental document.
4.2. Implementation details
Our multi-head baseline model is ViTPose++ [56]. We use
an input image size of 256×192 and apply a flip test in
all cases. The model is trained for 100 epochs using the
AdamW optimizer with a weight decay factor of 0.1 and
a step learning rate scheduler with an initial learning rate of
0.001. The learning rate is reduced by a factor of 0.1 at the
50th and 90th epochs. We use four NVIDIA A100 GPUs for
training with the ViT-H backbone and four NVIDIA A6000
GPUs for training with the ViT-B backbone.
We employ a scheduling strategy for both the losses and
the learning rate. Initially, we freeze the multi-head model,
updating only the embedding module and prototypes for 50
epochs. In the subsequent 40 epochs, we train the multi￾heads and the embedding module while keeping the proto￾types and the backbone frozen. Finally, during the last 10
epochs, we train the entire network except the prototypes.
At this stage, we introduce cross-type self-supervision loss.
This progressive optimization approach accelerates training
compared to training the entire network from the outset.

5. Conclusion
This paper presents a new multi-dataset training (MDT)
framework for pose estimation across diverse domains and
datasets. Traditional approaches encounter challenges with
label heterogeneity, dataset-specific parameters, and limited
domain generalization. Our approach addresses the limita￾tions with two main contributions. First, to resolve label
heterogeneity and dataset-specific parameters, we employ
nonparametric prototypes to unify diverse keypoint anno￾tations across multiple datasets. Second, to address the lim￾ited multi-dataset supervision, we propose a cross-type self￾supervision mechanism. The proposed method has demon￾strated substantial improvements over existing MDT across
a broad range of datasets, including human pose, animal
pose, hand shape, and human shape estimation.
Limitations and future work. A limitation of our ap￾proach is that it requires at least a few-shot sample to learn
prototypes, making zero-shot inference for unseen skele￾tons infeasible. Future work should explore extending our
method for zero-shot human pose estimation. Future work
should also investigate adapting the approach to 3D do￾mains e.g. using axis-angle rotation representations, to fa￾cilitate representation learning on 3D manifolds.

## 先问你第一个问题

你以前看的医学关键点论文，大多数是不是默认：

> 一个数据集里所有图像都标同一套关键点，比如 19 个头影测量点，或者 37 个手骨点？

> **如果一个数据集标 nose、eye、hip，另一个数据集也有 eye、hip，但一个是人，一个是狗；还有一个数据集标的是手指关节，这些点能不能直接合并成同一类关键点？**

作者的回答是：**不能简单合并。**

所以这篇论文真正要解决的是：

## 第二个问题：multi-head baseline 为什么不够？

普通做法是：

```text
共享 backbone
+ COCO head
+ MPII head
+ AIC head
+ AP-10K head
...
```

你可以把它想成：

> backbone 学公共视觉特征，每个数据集各用一个输出头。

这听起来合理，但作者认为它有个问题：

> **关键点输出空间没有共享。**

也就是说，COCO 的 left shoulder、MPII 的 shoulder、动物的 shoulder/head/hip 之间没有真正建立关系。它们只是共用 backbone，最后各管各的。

所以作者问了一个更进一步的问题：

> 能不能不只是共享 backbone，而是让“关键点本身”也进入一个统一空间？

这就是 PoseBH 的第一个核心：**Keypoint Prototype Learning**。

---

## 第三个问题：什么是 keypoint prototype？

你可以把 prototype 理解成：

> **每一种关键点语义在 embedding 空间里的“代表向量”或“中心”。**

医学关键点里你可以类比成：

```text
Sella 点有一个 prototype
Nasion 点有一个 prototype
ANS 点有一个 prototype
...
```

但 PoseBH 的场景更复杂，因为它不只是一个医学数据集，而是：

```text
COCO 的 nose
MPII 的 head
AIC 的 shoulder
AP-10K 动物的 eye
WholeBody 的 hand keypoints
...
```

它希望把这些关键点都放进一个统一 embedding 空间里。

具体做法是：

1. backbone 提取图像特征；
2. embedding head 输出每个像素的 keypoint embedding；
3. 每个关键点类别有若干 prototype；
4. 对每个像素，把它的 embedding 和所有 prototype 做余弦相似度；
5. 相似度最高的位置就是该关键点的 heatmap 响应。fileciteturn1file0

换句话说，传统 heatmap head 是：

```text
图像特征 → 直接输出关键点 heatmap
```

PoseBH 多加了一条训练路径：

```text
图像特征 → pixel embedding → 和 keypoint prototype 匹配 → prototype heatmap
```

---

## 第四个问题：为什么它要用“非参数 prototype”？

这里很关键。

作者不是把 prototype 当作普通可学习参数直接反向传播更新，而是采用一种 **nonparametric prototype update**。

你可以先想一个问题：

> 如果 prototype 是普通 learnable parameter，会不会又变成某个数据集专属的小模块？

作者想避免这个问题。因为他们的目标是：

> prototype 要能迁移到新骨架、新数据集，而不是死绑在某个固定 label set 上。

所以他们用前景关键点位置的 embedding 去更新 prototype，并用 Sinkhorn-Knopp 做在线分配，再用 momentum 更新 prototype。fileciteturn1file0

你可以粗略理解为：

```text
不是网络自己随便学 prototype
而是从真实关键点附近的 embedding 中聚类、更新 prototype
```

这有点像：

> 每种关键点不是一个死的类别编号，而是在特征空间中形成若干“视觉-语义簇”。

---

## 第五个问题：为什么一个关键点要多个 prototype？

论文里每个 keypoint class 不是只有一个 prototype，而是有 M 个 in-class prototypes。

你可以想想医学关键点：

> 同一个 Sella 点，在不同头颅形态、不同拍摄质量、不同遮挡/噪声下，外观可能一样吗？

不一样。

所以一个关键点类别用多个 prototype，可以表达：

```text
同一关键点的多种外观模式
同一语义下的多种局部形态
不同数据域下的差异
```

对于人/动物/手这种跨域更大的任务，这一点更重要。

---

## 第六个问题：这篇论文的第二个核心 CSS 在解决什么？

PoseBH 的第二个核心叫：

> **Cross-type Self-Supervision，跨类型自监督。**

它解决的是多数据集训练里的另一个问题：**标签稀疏。**

比如一张 COCO 图像，只标了 COCO 的 17 个点。

但是模型同时有：

```text
COCO head
AIC head
MPII head
AP-10K head
WholeBody head
...
```

那对这张 COCO 图像来说，AIC/MPII/动物/WholeBody 的输出有没有 ground truth？

没有。

所以作者的问题是：

> 一张图只给一种 skeleton 的标注，那其他 skeleton head 怎么监督？

普通方法可能不管它，或者用 teacher-student、augmentation、伪标签。PoseBH 的做法更巧妙：

它利用两种预测来源互相验证：

```text
dataset-specific keypoint head 的预测
prototype embedding 分支的预测
```

如果两个分支都很自信，而且预测位置接近，就认为这个点比较可靠，然后生成 pseudo heatmap 作为自监督标签。论文里用了两个过滤条件：置信度超过阈值，两个预测位置的 RMS 距离小于阈值。fileciteturn1file2

你可以把 CSS 理解成：

> **如果 head 也这么认为，prototype 也这么认为，而且两者位置差不多，那这个伪标签就可信。**

## 第七个问题：CSS 和你熟悉的医学关键点有什么关系？

这点对你很有启发。

医学关键点里，我们经常担心：

```text
某些点本身模糊
医生之间有分歧
模型预测不确定
```

PoseBH 不是做医生分歧，也不是 RLE 不确定性，但它有一个思想很有价值：

> **不要只相信单一路径的预测，而是让两个不同机制的预测互相筛选。**

在你的语境里，可以类比成：

```text
heatmap 分支预测
regression 分支预测
prototype 分支预测
uncertainty 分支预测
clinical constraint 分支预测
```

如果多个来源一致，就把它当作可靠监督；如果不一致，说明这个点可能困难、模糊或跨域不稳定。

## 第八个问题：训练完以后 inference 会不会变慢？

这是这篇论文很聪明的地方。

PoseBH 的 embedding head、prototype matching、CSS 主要用于训练阶段。推理时，论文图 2 明确表示只保留 backbone + 对应 dataset head，embedding head 和后续 prototype 流程不参与 inference。fileciteturn1file4

```text

推理时不改变主干结构或几乎不增加成本
```

## 第十二个问题：这篇论文的局限是什么？

作者自己也承认一个限制：

> 对于未见过的新 skeleton，不能 zero-shot 直接预测，至少需要 few-shot 样本来学习 prototype。fileciteturn1file3

也就是说，它不是：

```text
给一个新骨架定义，模型立刻会预测
```

而是：

```text
给一些新骨架样本
先学 prototype
再迁移
```

所以它的能力是 **few-shot transfer**，不是 **zero-shot keypoint generalization**。

Abstract

where skeletal heterogeneity presents a unique challenge

relies on dataset merging or multi-head supervision. How￾ever, the diversity of skeleton types and limited cross-dataset

2. Related works
Multi-dataset training has been widely explored in vari￾ous computer vision tasks. Relabeling and pseudo-labeling
are among the most straightforward yet resource-intensive
approaches. For example, MSeg [20] employed relabeling
for segmentation, while [68] used offline pseudo-labeling
for object detection. However, both methods face scalability
challenges, as adding a new dataset requires reprocessing
annotations for all previously processed datasets to redefine
the class set, leading to a quadratic computational cost.
Several approaches have been proposed to improve scal￾ability. In object detection, [4, 70] aim to unify object class
labels by merging similar categories. However, they strug￾gle to integrate localization features of the same object
due to the absence of spatial information in class logits.
UniTrack learns a unified identity embedding through self￾supervised learning for multiple object-tracking tasks, in￾cluding human pose tracking [51]. However, it prioritizes
pose propagation over estimation and relies exclusively on
the PoseTrack18 [2] skeleton, without incorporating multi￾ple pose datasets.
Another class of methods [50, 54, 58, 66] em￾ploys network modules, such as graph convolutional net￾works (GCN), for cross-dataset adaptation. However, these
dataset-specific modules limit domain transferability, as
their input and output sizes are dictated by the label struc￾ture, making them incompatible with datasets that have dif￾ferent keypoint formats. In contrast, our approach achieves
flexible transferability without relying on dataset-specific
domain adaptation modules.
Human pose estimation with MDT. In 3D human shape
estimation, several methods have used pseudo-ground truths
(GTs) generated by the SMPL model for MDT [5, 18,
43, 44]. However, these pseudo-GTs are often inaccu￾rate due to depth ambiguity in 2D images and the lim￾ited expressiveness of the parametric 3D model [30, 31].
Similar to [70], Sar´ andi et al.’s affine-combining autoen- ´
coder (ACAE) merges 3D joint coordinates via an autoen￾coder [37]. However, the autoencoder’s learnable parame￾ters are dataset-dependent, limiting its generalizability. Fur￾thermore, ACAE does not preserve pixel-level features,
making it unsuitable when spatial information is crucial.
Recent works on category-agnostic pose estimation inte￾grate various objects, such as chairs and vehicles, by com￾bining multiple datasets [14, 55]. These methods follow a
relabeling approach, creating a superset of keypoint classes
and detecting only the keypoints defined in their relabeled
dataset. As a result, they do not address keypoint hetero￾geneity or label sparsity issues, as training and evalua￾tion occur within a single labeled dataset. In contrast, our
method uses multiple skeletons to improve generalization
across diverse downstream domains.
In 2D human pose estimation, UniHCP performs mul￾tiple human perception tasks using task-specific queries
without incorporating task-specific layers inside the back￾bone [6]. This design limits its ability to generalize to dis￾tant domains such as animals and whole-body pose estima￾tion. ViTPose++ [56] and Sapiens [17] employ multi-head

## 启发 1：不要把两个医生标注简单平均，而要看成“同一个点的两个合理观测”

DuoAnno 现在的核心是：

```text
医生1标注 y1
医生2标注 y2
平均标注 y_mean
```

很多方法会直接用：

```text
y_mean = (y1 + y2) / 2
```

然后把 y_mean 当成唯一真值。

但 PoseBH 给你的启发是：
**一个关键点不一定只有一个固定表示，它可以有多个 prototype / 多种模式。**

PoseBH 里每个 keypoint class 有多个 in-class prototypes，用来表达同一类关键点内部的多样性；它不是把所有样本强行压成一个中心。fileciteturn4file0

对应到你的 DuoAnno：

> y1 和 y2 不应该只是被平均掉，它们可以被看成同一个解剖点的两个 annotation modes。

也就是说，对于第 j 个医学关键点，可以从：

```text
一个点 j → 一个目标 y_mean
```

改成：

```text
一个点 j → 两个医生观测 y1_j, y2_j
```

这就是你之前 DA-RLE 的思想来源之一：
**两个医生不是噪声，而是同一个真实解剖点的两个可能观测。**

## 启发 2：可以给每个医学点建立“解剖 prototype”，再用医生分歧更新它

PoseBH 的 prototype 是这样用的：

```text
pixel embedding 和 keypoint prototype 做匹配
得到 prototype heatmap
```

它想学一个统一 keypoint embedding space。fileciteturn4file4

你可以把这个思想搬到医学关键点里，但不用做得那么大。

比如每个医学点 j 维护一个 prototype：

```text
P_Sella
P_Nasion
P_ANS
...
```

训练时，你从医生标注附近取 embedding：

```text
e(y1_j)
e(y2_j)
```

然后让它们都靠近同一个解剖 prototype：

```text
e(y1_j) → P_j
e(y2_j) → P_j
```

这代表：

> 虽然两个医生坐标不完全一样，但它们都应该属于同一个解剖语义点。

这对 DuoAnno 很有用，因为它能避免模型只学坐标平均，而是学：

```text
这个区域为什么是 Sella
这个区域为什么是 Nasion
```

也就是从“坐标监督”升级成“解剖语义表示监督”。

## 启发 3：医生一致性可以变成一种“可靠性过滤”

PoseBH 的 CSS 很关键。它不是随便用伪标签，而是要求：

```text
keypoint head 预测自信
prototype head 预测自信

```

满足这些条件，才生成 reliable heatmap。fileciteturn4file1

这对 DuoAnno 的启发非常直接。

你的两个医生其实天然提供了一个可靠性信号：

```text
如果 y1 和 y2 很近：

如果 y1 和 y2 很远：
这个点本身模糊，不能强迫模型过度贴近某一个医生
```

所以 DuoAnno 可以从原来的：

```text

```

```text
医生一致点：强监督定位
医生分歧点：弱化定位强度，同时增强 σ / uncertainty 学习
```

这和 PoseBH 的 mutual filtering 是同一种思想：

> **一致性高的地方当作可靠监督，不一致的地方当作不确定区域。**

## 最适合 DuoAnno 的一个新 idea：Prototype-DuoAnno

我觉得最自然的改法不是大改网络，而是加一个训练期辅助模块：

```text
主干模型仍然输出 μ 和 σ
额外加一个 embedding head
每个医学关键点维护若干 prototype
训练时用 y1/y2 附近 embedding 更新 prototype
推理时可以去掉 embedding head
```

结构可以这样理解：

```text
Image
→ Backbone
→ Regression head: μ, σ
→ Embedding head: pixel embedding
→ Landmark prototypes: P_j
```

训练时有三类 loss：

### 第一类：双医生 RLE 定位损失

不是只对 mean 做 RLE，而是：

```text
L_RLE = NLL(y1 | μ, σ) + NLL(y2 | μ, σ)
```

含义是：

> μ、σ 要同时解释两个医生标注。

如果 y1 和 y2 很近，模型会学小 σ；
如果 y1 和 y2 很远，模型自然需要更大 σ 才能覆盖两个观测。

这比简单 rank loss 更自然，因为它直接有概率解释。

---

### 第二类：prototype 解剖语义损失

对第 j 个关键点：

```text
e(y1_j) 靠近 P_j
e(y2_j) 靠近 P_j
其他关键点 embedding 远离 P_j
```

含义是：

> 医生1和医生2虽然点的位置略有差异，但它们都应该落在同一个解剖 landmark 的语义区域内。

这能补强 DuoAnno 的表示能力。

---

### 第三类：医生分歧校准损失

用医生距离：

```text
d_j = || y1_j - y2_j ||
```

去约束模型不确定性：

```text
σ_j 应该和 d_j 正相关
```

但这里要小心，不要像 DCRLE-rank 那样只做排序，否则容易变成 calibration-only，对定位帮助不明显。

更稳的做法是：

```text
RLE 主导定位
prototype 主导解剖表示
disagreement calibration 辅助 σ
```

---

## 这个 idea 和原 DuoAnno 的区别

原 DuoAnno 更像：

```text
我有两个医生标注，所以我用两个坐标监督模型
```

Prototype-DuoAnno 更像：

```text
我有两个医生标注，所以我要学习：
1. 同一解剖点的共同语义
2. 医生分歧带来的不确定性
3. 哪些点可靠，哪些点模糊
```

区别很大。

原来是“多一个标签”；
现在是“多一个可靠性/语义结构”。
